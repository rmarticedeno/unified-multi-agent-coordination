\chapter{Conclusions and Future Work}
\label{chap:conclusion}

The thesis proposes a protocol-adaptable boundary between linguistic proposal and executable multi-agent coordination. RQ1 is answered at the design and prototype level: canonical capabilities, typed constraints, task dependencies, authority, validation and completion contracts, artifacts, attempts, and persisted trace identity must be explicit to justify dispatch. RQ2 is answered architecturally: language interprets and proposes, while deterministic components resolve identity, evaluate enforceable predicates, authorize, refuse, and record. RQ3 is answered through bounded task-local auxiliary specifications whose method, schema, validator, read-only authority, and lifecycle are explicit.

RQ4 is not answered from the tuned v0.4 development cycle. Version 0.3 established a negative all-refusal baseline and v0.4 demonstrated improvement with unresolved small-model robustness. The final answer is deliberately delegated to the first complete clean v0.5 dual-arm matrix, whose outcome is reportable whether positive, mixed, or negative. No independent adjudication or broad superiority is claimed.

RQ5 is specified as a second core evaluation track. The implementation supplies consensus-backed authoritative storage, generation-CAS configuration, resumable sequential membership operations, learner-first promotion, fencing, linearizable authorization reads, fail-closed quorum responses, and a repeatable 3/5/7 fault campaign. Historical three-voter evidence supports only selected formation, registry, ownership, authentication, terminal, and duplicate-suppression checks. Because the full campaign has not yet completed from a clean revision, RQ5 is not empirically answered in this manuscript state. In particular, no claim is made for multi-host availability, Byzantine tolerance, automatic recovery after majority loss, or exactly-once external effects.

The principal scientific claim is therefore deliberately narrow. The implementation demonstrates how established symbolic, security, persistence, interoperability, and crash-consensus mechanisms can be integrated so that unsupported linguistic proposals are refused before action and authoritative mutations stop when quorum is unavailable. Duplicate external effects are suppressed only when receivers enforce both operation keys and monotonic fences. The work does not prove semantic truth, production safety, malicious-agent resistance, decentralized authorization, Byzantine tolerance, exactly-once effects, or universal protocol interoperability.

Before submission, the operational work is to freeze the non-final evidence-candidate commit; collect and report the first complete v0.5 matrix without selection; execute the implemented 33-trial fault campaign from the same source and one immutable image; promote final immutable Docker A2A and PostgreSQL evidence; validate every evidence hash; and rebuild the final PDF. Only after those technical gates close should the manifest become \texttt{technical-ready-administrative-blocked}. Institutional names, evaluated-source and packaging commit metadata, tagging, pushing, and submission packaging remain the manual handoff. Longer-term research should improve small-model draft robustness, reduce repair dependence, add independent corpus adjudication, mTLS with per-node identities and rotation, adversarial registries, external domain agents, semantic validators, replanning, independent voter failure domains, snapshot and disaster-recovery procedures, and broader deployments.
