\chapter{Evaluation and Results}
\label{chap:results}

This chapter leads with the frozen version~0.7 production-path experiment. Developmental qualification, historical studies, and distributed evidence are reported separately so that reproducibility is not confused with a favorable outcome. Failed gates, unsafe acceptances, model-server errors, infrastructure timeouts, and unexecuted checks remain visible.

\section{Evidence Status and Accounting}

Version~0.7 contains 64 author-labelled, label-hidden cases arranged as 32 feasible/infeasible matched pairs. Three local models, seeds 11 and 29, and independently prompted hybrid and direct-LLM arms produce 768 expected observations. All 768 identities are present. One Qwen3-1.7B direct-arm observation at seed 29 ended in a model-server error and is scored as a failed observation rather than semantically retried. Gemma-4-E2B and Qwen3-8B completed without server errors.

The production source, corpus, prompts, schemas, model parameters, analysis code, gates, and runtime identities were frozen before confirmatory collection. \subsection{Semantic Fields and Predeclared Gates}

The supplementary descriptive scorer was added after the behavioral freeze to aggregate field-level booleans already present in the frozen analysis. It does not change decisions. Hybrid primary-seed exact accuracy is 92.19\% for goals, 95.31\% for trust policy, 97.92\% for artifact contracts, 86.98\% for forbidden capabilities, 94.27\% for forbidden agents, and 83.33\% for unresolved terms. The corresponding F1 values are 91.46\%, 96.32\%, 98.95\%, 18.18\%, 65.88\%, and 35.82\%. Status accuracy is 52.60\% with macro-F1 31.57\%. High exact accuracy on sparse exclusion fields therefore coexists with weak positive-class F1 and should not be read as reliable ambiguity handling.

Only two of six declared gates pass:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item zero primary-seed unsafe hybrid acceptances: \textbf{fail} (one);
  \item at least 75\% feasible recall overall and 60\% per model: \textbf{fail};
  \item safety superiority over direct LLM: \textbf{pass};
  \item feasible-recall non-inferiority within 10 points: \textbf{fail};
  \item superiority over exact-name/alias matching: \textbf{fail};
  \item 100\% oracle/compiler invariants: \textbf{pass}.
\end{enumerate}
RQ4 is therefore supported only for relative safety and deterministic invariants, not for the proposed utility, zero-unsafe, non-inferiority, or lexical-superiority claims.

\section{Qwen-First Development Method}

Before freezing, 48 development-only cases covered paraphrase, ambiguity, negation, trust, artifact contracts, dependencies, provider recovery, and adversarial wording. Qwen3-1.7B was run first on both arms at seeds 11 and 29, producing 192 outputs per qualification run. The initial run exposed universal refusal. Later runs reached hybrid balanced accuracy of 81.25\% and 75.00\%, but still produced one unsafe acceptance and feasible recall of 66.67\% and 54.17\%. These values motivated prompt and implementation corrections and are excluded from confirmatory claims.

Qualification also exercised registry permutations, missing providers, malformed intent, alias collisions, explicit negation, prompt injection, and bounded-search exhaustion. The phase ended only after complete output accounting, no unhandled exception, fail-closed invalid inputs, and deterministic compiler invariants. The larger models were not used to tune the frozen protocol.

\section{Version 0.8 Development Qualification}

The v0.8 development matrix is complete at \VEightDevelopmentOutputCount{} observations: 72 cases, two permitted models, two arms, and two requested seeds. Qwen3-8B was not loaded and has no v0.8 observation. Both Qwen3-1.7B and Gemma-4-E2B passed every predeclared qualification gate. At primary seed 11, the pooled hybrid achieved \VEightDevelopmentHybridBalancedAccuracy\% balanced accuracy, \VEightDevelopmentHybridRecall\% feasible recall, \VEightDevelopmentHybridUnsafe{} unsafe acceptances, 100\% specificity, and 100\% schema validity. Every model/category feasible-recall cell was 100\%, and derived-status macro-F1 was \VEightDevelopmentStatusMacroFOne\%.

The direct arm retained complete feasible recall but achieved \VEightDevelopmentDirectBalancedAccuracy\% pooled balanced accuracy and made \VEightDevelopmentDirectUnsafe{} unsafe acceptances at the primary seed. Direct proposals were translated to the shared proposal type and checked with the same feasibility analyzer; this validation does not change the model's accept/refuse decision, but identifies whether an accepted proposal satisfies the common symbolic predicates.

These figures are qualification history, not held-out evidence. Earlier v0.8 development iterations are retained, including an initial universal-refusal run and a later run that failed category recall floors. The final values therefore demonstrate that the implementation reached its entry criteria on the data used for revision; they cannot estimate generalization. The 96-case confirmatory labels remain unopened because the required two temporally separated human author passes have not yet been certified. Consequently, no v0.8 confirmatory result or v0.7--v0.8 performance claim is made here.

\section{Consensus-v4 Distributed Evidence}

The complete campaign contains 45 trials: 42 primary trials over 14 conditions plus three supplementary combined-fault trials. Forty-three trials passed. One $3\rightarrow5\rightarrow3$ and one $5\rightarrow7\rightarrow5$ trial timed out before their five checks executed; the other two repetitions of each reconfiguration passed. No executed primary safety check failed: 140 of 150 executed, with ten unexecuted because of those infrastructure timeouts. All 27 supplementary checks executed and passed. The complete total is therefore 167/177 executed checks with zero observed violations.

RQ5 is partially supported for the conditions whose three repetitions completed and remains indeterminate for complete reconfiguration reliability.

\section{Historical Motivation and Synthesis}

Versions~0.3 and~0.4 remain developmental evidence. Version~0.3 exposed universal feasible refusal by the hybrid; version~0.4 improved the constrained bridge but failed its per-model recall floor. The frozen v0.5 comparison then produced 1,440 outputs: the repaired hybrid achieved 50.69\% accuracy, 1.39\% feasible recall, and zero unsafe acceptances, while the direct coordinator achieved 52.08\% accuracy, complete feasible recall, and 345 unsafe acceptances. That valid negative result motivated the production semantic-admission redesign. The unexecuted study-specific v0.6 protocol is marked superseded rather than presented as evidence.

Version~0.7 demonstrates a substantial increase in hybrid utility relative to v0.5 while retaining far stronger safety than direct prompting. It nevertheless fails four of six predeclared gates, includes one unsafe ambiguity acceptance, and loses to an exact-alias control tailored to the corpus. The correct conclusion is consequently narrower than ``hybrid superiority'': deterministic admission and compilation make model proposals auditable and much safer, but useful, general semantic admission remains model-sensitive and unproven.
