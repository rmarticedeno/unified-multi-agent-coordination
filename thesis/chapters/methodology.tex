\chapter{Research Methodology}
\label{chap:methodology}

\section{Study Design}

The thesis follows design science: it constructs a protocol-adaptable coordination boundary and evaluates whether the implementation exhibits the declared authorization, refusal, execution, and recovery behavior. The unit of analysis is one coordination attempt, comprising the natural-language request, admitted registry snapshot, typed request, non-authoritative proposal, symbolic predicates, authorization decision, task attempts, artifacts, and persisted trace. Outcome correctness and process correctness are analyzed separately.

The evaluation is staged. Unit and integration tests exercise individual contracts and transitions. Seven deterministic cases exercise the complete in-process workflow. Container harnesses exercise A2A admission and dispatch across processes and lease-backed recovery through PostgreSQL. The main comparative study uses a versioned corpus of 36 cases and local LLM proposals. Results from an earlier implementation are classified as legacy and cannot supply numerical claims for version 0.2.

\section{Versioned, Pre-registered, and Label-hidden Corpus}

The version 0.3 corpus contains 24 base cases, four in each of six strata: direct feasible execution; decomposed feasible execution; feasible planning followed by runtime failure; missing capability or auxiliary overreach; schema, modality, graph, or ordering invalidity; and authority, typed-constraint, availability, or registry invalidity. Twelve controlled variations add four paraphrases, four availability changes, and four capability-alias perturbations. Public requests, registry snapshots, payloads, and shared proposals are stored separately from hidden reference labels.

Version 0.4 is a new evidence cycle created after observing the version 0.3 all-refusal result. It does not alter or retrospectively rescore version 0.3. It preserves 24 base cases and 12 controlled variations as the 36 held-out statistical units, replaces generic request language with six domain-specific settings, and places six explicitly unscored development examples outside the held-out matrix. Its pre-registered success conditions are zero hybrid false acceptances, majority acceptance for at least 12 of the 15 feasible case units, and feasible-case recall of at least 60\% for every model. Because both corpus wording and bridge protocol change, comparison with version 0.3 is a sequential redesign evaluation, not a paired replication.

The thesis author defined the reference labels and minimum justifications before model collection. A provenance record identifies the author, annotation date, labeling protocol, corpus version and SHA-256 digest, and declares the labels frozen. No independent adjudicator was available. Reference labels are never included in model prompts and are loaded by the scorer only after collection is complete. An automated audit checks document shape, unique identities, public/hidden alignment, hash agreement, status consistency, and justification presence; it establishes internal consistency, not independent correctness. Any post-freeze change requires a new corpus version and hash.

\section{Configurations and Fairness}

Each applicable raw proposal is evaluated under: the full hybrid coordinator; a natural-language rule matcher; a structured-request rule-only oracle, explicitly interpreted as an upper bound; an LLM-only tool-aware baseline without a plan-level symbolic gate; and six hybrid ablations removing typed constraints, authority, contract validation, dependency gating, bounded auxiliary rules, or durable trace recording. Configurations receive the same request, registry snapshot, payload, and raw linguistic output where applicable. A baseline is not granted additional agents, credentials, hidden labels, or privileged registry information.

Planning-only decisions and runtime compliance are not conflated. Dependency-gating and durable-trace ablations may leave the planning decision unchanged while altering execution safety or observability. Those mechanisms are therefore scored through dispatch, skip, duplicate-effect, and trace-completeness measures rather than through feasibility accuracy alone.

The runtime-only study contains five dependency scenarios, five auxiliary-admission scenarios, and five trace-evidence scenarios, each repeated ten times under the applicable strict and negative-control configurations. This produces 350 deterministic observations in a separate immutable bundle. The dependency control bypasses predecessor status; the auxiliary controls disable the feature or bypass its bounds; and the trace control suppresses returned diagnostic evidence while preserving state-critical persistence. These rows are not treated as LLM planning observations.

\section{Local Model Protocol}

The fixed local models are \path{qwen/qwen3-1.7b}, \path{google/gemma-4-e2b}, and \path{qwen/qwen3-8b}. Qwen 14B is excluded because its installed nine-gigabyte quantization does not leave a reproducible operating margin on the 16~GB host. LM Studio is addressed at \url{http://127.0.0.1:1234/v1} with a non-secret local API-key value. Temperature is zero and seeds are 11, 22, 33, 44, and 55.

All other models are unloaded before a batch. The runner loads one exact identifier, verifies the model catalog, sends a sentinel request, and then runs all 36 cases for each seed before changing models. The prompt requests a typed request and between one and three typed candidate plans, with a fixed 4096-token output ceiling. Every response preserves the prompt, raw response, parsed object, returned model identifier, token metadata, latency, seed, and decoding parameters. This yields 540 immutable model outputs. Repeated identical outputs remain in the evidence set. Interrupted runs may resume only after corpus and existing-record identity checks; completed case files are never rewritten.

For version 0.4 the complete admitted \texttt{ProblemRequest} and registry snapshot are public prompt inputs, while the output is only a \texttt{LinguisticPlanDraft}. Deterministic hydration occurs after model output. When the draft contains repairable structural errors---such as omitted, duplicate, or unknown requirement references or invalid dependencies---the model receives those public errors once and may return one repaired draft. Capability unavailability and symbolic infeasibility do not trigger repair. Initial linguistic latency, repair latency, symbolic-validation latency, execution latency, and total latency are stored separately.

\section{Metrics and Statistical Analysis}

Decision measures include accuracy, precision, recall, F1, false acceptance, false refusal, status agreement, and category-level errors. Boundary measures include constraint, authority, schema, modality, dependency, and evidence rejection; artifact-contract validity; required-artifact completion; authorization-before-dispatch; dependent-task skipping; capability-ID resolution; unresolved references; and trace completeness and isolation. Efficiency measures include messages, planner calls, tokens, median and interquartile latency, and coordination overhead. Recovery measures include duplicate dispatch, repeated effect, stale-write rejection, recovery latency, and terminal correctness.

The 36 cases are the primary statistical units; model and seed outputs are repeated measurements. Results are stratified by model and seed. Exact paired McNemar tests compare decision errors within strata, with multiplicity correction. Difference in proportions reports effect magnitude, and a case-clustered fixed-seed bootstrap reports 95\% uncertainty for accuracy. Linguistic, symbolic-validation, execution, and total latency are recorded separately when applicable. No general superiority claim is made when the designed sample or statistical assumptions do not justify it.

\section{Provenance and Reproducibility}

Each run directory records Git SHA, dirty-state flag, corpus hash, model ID, prompt/schema version, seed, dependency-lock hash, Docker image digest, Python, PostgreSQL, A2A and LM Studio versions, operating system, CPU, RAM, and available accelerator information. Reports are written to new run-ID directories; convenience summaries are derived and may be regenerated, but the only copy of a raw run is never overwritten.

The release gates require all unit and integration tests, static lint and type checks, the seven deterministic cases, eleven Docker A2A checks, five distributed checks, the third-party A2A check, evidence-schema checks, and LuaLaTeX/BibTeX compilation. Coverage thresholds are 85\% overall, 90\% for feasibility, coordination execution, and trace code, and 80\% for the PostgreSQL store and evaluation runners. A release cannot be frozen while any threshold, frozen-label provenance check, institutional metadata, or final-commit field is open.

\section{Validity Boundaries}

The corpus is designed and labelled by the thesis author rather than sampled from a deployment population or independently adjudicated. This creates researcher-bias, construct-circularity, and baseline-design threats: agreement measures conformance to the declared framework criteria, not neutral benchmark truth. Freezing labels before collection and hiding them from prompts reduces leakage but does not create independence. The agents are mostly controlled fixtures, and the local models are small. Contract validity does not establish semantic truth. The PostgreSQL lease-and-fencing evidence concerns measured crash faults and is not consensus evidence. The later etcd harness exercises consensus-backed state in one three-voter Docker topology, but it is post-study engineering validation rather than part of the frozen comparative experiment. A2A admission establishes local origin, transport, trust, and credential policy; it does not establish cryptographic identity or malicious-agent resistance. These boundaries constrain every answer to the research questions.
