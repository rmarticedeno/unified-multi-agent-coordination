\chapter{Research Methodology}
\label{chap:methodology}

This chapter describes the design-science method used to construct and evaluate the proposed coordination boundary. Its goal is to translate the research questions into observable units, controlled comparisons, fault scenarios, metrics, and release criteria while keeping planning quality, runtime safety, and distributed recovery as distinct forms of evidence.

The chapter is organized as follows. The study-design section defines the unit of analysis and the staged evaluation. The corpus section explains the versioned, pre-specified, and label-hidden cases. The configurations-and-fairness section defines the hybrid, direct-LLM, deterministic, and ablation controls. The local-model protocol then specifies collection, followed by the statistical measures and analysis procedure. The consensus campaign section defines the separate crash-fault evaluation, and the final sections establish provenance, reproducibility, and validity boundaries. Disclosure of AI systems used as experimental subjects or research assistants is provided in Appendix~\ref{app:ai-disclosure}.

\section{Study Design}

The thesis follows design science: it constructs a protocol-adaptable coordination boundary and evaluates whether the implementation exhibits the declared authorization, refusal, execution, and recovery behavior. The unit of analysis is one coordination attempt, comprising the natural-language request, admitted registry snapshot, typed request, non-authoritative proposal, symbolic predicates, authorization decision, task attempts, artifacts, and persisted trace. Outcome correctness and process correctness are analyzed separately.

The evaluation is staged. Unit and integration tests exercise individual contracts and transitions. Deterministic cases exercise the complete in-process workflow, bounded provider search, registry permutations, alternative-provider recovery, and refusal beyond the assignment limit. Container harnesses exercise A2A admission and dispatch across processes and lease-backed or consensus-backed recovery. The primary comparative study is version~0.7: 64 held-out cases arranged as 32 feasible/infeasible matched pairs, three models, two requested seeds, and two independently prompted arms, producing 768 confirmatory outputs. Version~0.5 is retained as the experiment that motivated the production-path redesign; versions~0.3 and~0.4 remain earlier development evidence, and the unexecuted study-specific v0.6 protocol is superseded.

\section{Versioned, Pre-specified, and Label-hidden Corpus}

The version 0.3 corpus contains 24 base cases, four in each of six strata: direct feasible execution; decomposed feasible execution; feasible planning followed by runtime failure; missing capability or auxiliary overreach; schema, modality, graph, or ordering invalidity; and authority, typed-constraint, availability, or registry invalidity. Twelve controlled variations add four paraphrases, four availability changes, and four capability-alias perturbations. Public requests, registry snapshots, payloads, and shared proposals are stored separately from hidden reference labels.

Version 0.4 was a development cycle created after observing the version 0.3 all-refusal result. It is not retrospectively altered. Because its corpus language and bridge protocol were used to tune the redesign, it is historical rather than confirmatory evidence.

Version 0.5 is fresh and was frozen before collection. Eight domains each contribute three matched pairs, and cyclic offsets assign three pairs to each of eight invalidity categories: missing capability, unavailable agent, insufficient trust, incompatible modality, typed-constraint violation, invalid artifact contract, dependency/order error, and auxiliary overreach. The resulting 48 held-out cases are distinct from eight unscored development examples. Twelve additional runtime cases test dependency failure, timeout and unknown attempts, retry/recovery, artifact rejection, and trace behavior without entering planning accuracy. The protocol is described as \emph{pre-specified}, not preregistered, because it was not deposited in an external timestamped registry.

Version 0.6 was drafted for the intermediate study-specific bridge but was never executed. Its manifest is preserved and marks it superseded rather than silently deleting or rewriting it.

Version 0.7 evaluates the production semantic-admission and symbolic-compilation path. A 48-case development-only corpus contains six cases in each of eight strata: paraphrase, ambiguity, negation, trust, artifact contracts, dependencies, provider recovery, and adversarial wording. It is used first with Qwen3-1.7B to expose implementation and prompt defects; all developmental runs, including failed qualifications, are retained, but their accuracy is not confirmatory evidence. The frozen corpus contains 64 held-out cases as 32 feasible/infeasible matched pairs, with four pairs in each stratum. Public cases and author-only labels are separated. The freeze records hashes of the corpus, prompt, schemas, production source, analysis, benchmark, model parameters, dependency lock, and runtime identities.

The thesis author defined the reference labels and minimum justifications before model collection. A provenance record identifies the author, annotation date, labeling protocol, corpus version and SHA-256 digest, and declares the labels frozen. No independent adjudicator was available. Reference labels are never included in model prompts and are loaded by the scorer only after collection is complete. An automated audit checks document shape, unique identities, public/hidden alignment, hash agreement, status consistency, and justification presence; it establishes internal consistency, not independent correctness. Any post-freeze change requires a new corpus version and hash.

\section{Configurations and Fairness}

For every model, seed, and held-out case, two arms receive the same public natural-language request, admitted catalog, registry, and output-schema budget. The production hybrid arm emits a strict semantic selection that remains non-authoritative; deterministic admission constructs the typed request and the shared compiler makes the authorization decision. The direct-LLM arm emits an explicit accept/refuse decision without a plan-level symbolic authorization gate. Its decision is scored offline and is never dispatched. Both arms receive one call, temperature 0.2, top-p 1, and an 800-token limit. Invalid schema is a failed observation without semantic retry; transport and model-server failures are classified separately.

An exact-name/alias baseline admits only uniquely grounded literal catalog terms. An oracle semantic-admission baseline supplies the author-labelled semantic intent to the same deterministic compiler, and a first-provider ablation removes ranked alternative-provider recovery. These controls distinguish semantic admission from symbolic compilation and provider selection. Registry-order permutations and the 4,096-assignment refusal boundary are evaluated deterministically.

Planning-only decisions and runtime compliance are not conflated. Dependency-gating and durable-trace ablations may leave the planning decision unchanged while altering execution safety or observability. Those mechanisms are therefore scored through dispatch, skip, duplicate-effect, and trace-completeness measures rather than through feasibility accuracy alone.

The runtime-only study contains five dependency scenarios, five auxiliary-admission scenarios, and five trace-evidence scenarios, each repeated ten times under the applicable strict and negative-control configurations. This produces 350 deterministic observations in a separate immutable bundle. The dependency control bypasses predecessor status; the auxiliary controls disable the feature or bypass its bounds; and the trace control suppresses returned diagnostic evidence while preserving state-critical persistence. These rows are not treated as LLM planning observations.

\section{Local Model Protocol}

The fixed local models are \path{qwen/qwen3-1.7b}, \path{google/gemma-4-e2b}, and \path{qwen/qwen3-8b}. LM Studio is addressed at \url{http://127.0.0.1:1234/v1} with a non-secret local API-key value. Temperature is 0.2, top-p is 1, the maximum response is 800 tokens, and requested seeds are 11 and 29. Seed 11 is primary and seed 29 is a stability replication rather than an independent sample.

All other models are unloaded before a batch. The runner loads one exact identifier, uses 16,384-token context and parallelism one, and verifies the model catalog. Qwen3-1.7B is collected first. Only runtime completeness and artifact integrity are checked before Gemma-4-E2B and Qwen3-8B run under the identical protocol; poor Qwen performance cannot trigger tuning or stop the sequence. For each of 64 cases, both arms are collected at two requested seeds for each of three models, yielding exactly 768 immutable confirmatory outputs. Every record preserves arm/model/seed/case identity, prompt, raw response, parsed object or schema failure, latency, token counts, and call count. Interrupted runs may resume only after corpus, protocol-hash, and existing-record identity checks; completed case files are never rewritten and scoring is blocked until the complete matrix exists.

For version 0.4 the complete admitted \texttt{ProblemRequest} and registry snapshot are public prompt inputs, while the output is only a \texttt{LinguisticPlanDraft}. Deterministic hydration occurs after model output. When the draft contains repairable structural errors---such as omitted, duplicate, or unknown requirement references or invalid dependencies---the model receives those public errors once and may return one repaired draft. Capability unavailability and symbolic infeasibility do not trigger repair. Initial linguistic latency, repair latency, symbolic-validation latency, execution latency, and total latency are stored separately.

\section{Qwen-First Development and Protocol Freeze}

Development begins with Qwen3-1.7B because it is the least expensive model in the fixed set and provides broad feedback before larger-model collection. Its 48 development cases exercise catalog-size variation, registry permutations, missing providers, malformed intent, alias collisions, prompt injection, ambiguous and negated language, and search exhaustion under both the hybrid and direct arms at seeds 11 and 29. Prompt and implementation corrections are permitted in this phase. Qualification requires complete output accounting, no unhandled exceptions, fail-closed ambiguous or invalid input, and 100\% deterministic compiler invariants. Developmental accuracy is reported only as process history.

After qualification, the protocol lock freezes production source hashes, corpus and labels, prompts, schemas, model parameters, analysis and benchmark code, success criteria, dependency lock, and runtime identity. Any behavioral correction after this point requires a new version and a complete three-model rerun. The author-only labels are disclosed as a limitation rather than described as independent ground truth.

\section{Metrics and Statistical Analysis}

Primary semantic measures are field-level accuracy and F1. Decision measures are balanced accuracy, unsafe acceptance, feasible recall, false refusal, plan validity, provider recovery, model/category errors, and seed agreement. Efficiency measures include latency, calls, prompt tokens, and completion tokens. Deterministic and distributed measures include topological-order and registry-permutation invariants, bounded-search refusal, authorization before dispatch, duplicate dispatch, repeated effects, stale-operation rejection, crash recovery, and terminal correctness.

The 32 matched pairs are the primary statistical units; model and requested-seed outputs are repeated measurements. Seed 11 supplies the primary estimates, while seed 29 measures stability. Matched-pair clustered bootstrap intervals are reported for hybrid-minus-direct unsafe acceptance and feasible recall, and for hybrid-minus-exact-alias balanced accuracy. Results are reported separately for every model and category rather than pooled into a single model-independent claim. Functional results and artifact identities are hashable; wall-clock latency remains hardware-dependent evidence.

The predeclared gates are zero primary-seed unsafe hybrid acceptances; at least 75\% feasible recall overall and 60\% for every model; safety superiority over the direct LLM; feasible-recall non-inferiority within ten percentage points; superiority over the exact-name/alias baseline; and 100\% oracle/compiler invariants. Every gate is reported, including negative outcomes, and no failed gate is tuned away after freeze.

\section{Consensus Crash-Fault Campaign}

RQ5 is evaluated as a separate core track rather than as additional LLM observations. Before accepted execution, the campaign protocol fixes three fresh trials for clean formation at voter targets three, five, and seven; live transitions $3\rightarrow5\rightarrow3$ and $5\rightarrow7\rightarrow5$; leader termination; minority partition; majority loss and restoration; persistent restart; failed-voter replacement; unavailable audit projection; concurrent ownership; and crashes after attempt start, after external dispatch, and during aggregation. Every trial uses a new Docker Compose project, new volumes except where persistence is the object of the test, and a non-existing evidence directory.

Safety observations are one committed session owner, monotonic fencing, rejection of stale writes, attempt start before completion, at most one terminal result, refusal of authoritative mutation without quorum, serialized membership changes, and zero repeated external effects when the receiving fixture enforces operation keys and fences. Conditional liveness requires leader-failure progress within 30 seconds while a majority survives, quorum-loss detection within 10 seconds, restored progress within 30 seconds, and membership convergence within 120 seconds. Steady state additionally requires authoritative role agreement and no pending membership operation. Repetition tests implementation stability but does not create independent deployment populations.

The campaign invokes etcd~3.6.13 as the Raft implementation; it does not reimplement or re-prove Raft. The fault model is crash/omission under eventual synchrony with a surviving majority. All containers share one Docker host, so the campaign does not represent independent machines, disks, regions, switches, or Byzantine behavior.

\section{Provenance and Reproducibility}

Each run directory records Git SHA, dirty-state flag, corpus hash, model ID, prompt/schema version, seed, dependency-lock hash, Docker image digest, Python, PostgreSQL, A2A and LM Studio versions, operating system, CPU, RAM, and available accelerator information. Reports are written to new run-ID directories; convenience summaries are derived and may be regenerated, but the only copy of a raw run is never overwritten.

The release gates require all unit and integration tests, static lint and type checks, the deterministic compiler benchmark, eight fixture-A2A replays, the PostgreSQL and upstream-derived A2A checks, the full consensus campaign, protocol and evidence-schema checks, and LuaLaTeX/BibTeX compilation. Coverage thresholds are 85\% overall production code; 90\% for semantic admission, symbolic compilation, feasibility, coordination execution, trace validation, plan hydration, and evidence preflight; 85\% for cluster discovery, launcher, etcd, registry, service, and SDK modules; and 80\% for PostgreSQL, the consensus campaign driver, and current evaluation modules. Fixture servers and system drivers are exercised as subprocesses or containers rather than counted in the production aggregate. A release cannot be frozen while any threshold, clean-source evidence, institutional metadata, evaluated-source commit, or packaging-commit field is open.

\section{Validity Boundaries}

The corpus is designed and labelled by the thesis author rather than sampled from a deployment population or independently adjudicated. This creates researcher-bias, construct-circularity, and baseline-design threats: agreement measures conformance to the declared framework criteria, not neutral benchmark truth. Freezing labels before collection and hiding them from prompts reduces leakage but does not create independence. The agents are mostly controlled fixtures, and the local models are small. Contract validity does not establish semantic truth. The PostgreSQL lease-and-fencing evidence concerns measured crash faults and is not consensus evidence. The etcd campaign is a core implementation evaluation but remains a single-host, project-authored fault campaign rather than proof of all Raft properties or multi-host availability. A2A admission establishes local origin, transport, trust, and credential policy; it does not establish cryptographic identity or malicious-agent resistance. These boundaries constrain every answer to the research questions.
